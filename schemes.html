Government Schemes for Farmers

Discover and access various government schemes designed to support farmers, enhance agricultural productivity, and improve livelihoods.

Central Government Schemes

Pradhan Mantri Kisan Samman Nidhi (PM-KISAN)

Objective: Direct income support of ₹6,000 per year to landholding farmer families.

Beneficiaries: Landholding farmer families across India.

Details: Disbursed in three equal installments of ₹2,000. Requires eKYC and Aadhaar-bank linking.

Learn More & Apply

Kisan Credit Card (KCC) Scheme

Objective: Provide timely and adequate credit support for cultivation and other agricultural needs.

Beneficiaries: Individual farmers, SHGs, JLGs (including tenant farmers and sharecroppers).

Details: Offers loans typically up to ₹3 lakh at low interest rates. Includes revolving cash credit and often insurance coverage.

Learn More & Apply

Pradhan Mantri Fasal Bima Yojana (PMFBY)

Objective: Financial protection against crop loss/damage due to natural disasters, pests, and diseases.

Beneficiaries: Cultivators or sharecroppers on insured land (compulsory for loanee farmers, optional for non-loanee).

Details: Affordable premiums (2% Kharif, 1.5% Rabi, 5% commercial/horticultural). Covers prevented sowing, standing crop, post-harvest losses, and localized calamities.

Learn More & Apply

Agriculture Infrastructure Fund (AIF)

Objective: Support creation of post-harvest management infrastructure and community farming assets.

Beneficiaries: Farmers, agribusiness startups, rural entrepreneurs.

Details: Provides loans up to ₹2 crore with a 3% interest subvention for warehouses, cold storage, food processing units.

Learn More & Apply

National Agriculture Market (e-NAM)

Objective: Online digital trading platform to facilitate direct selling for farmers and FPOs.

Beneficiaries: Farmers and Farmer Producer Organizations (FPOs).

Details: Aims to eliminate middlemen and ensure better, more transparent pricing for crops.

Learn More & Apply

Paramparagat Krishi Vikas Yojana (PKVY)

Objective: Comprehensive support for organic farmers from production to marketing in a cluster-based approach.

Beneficiaries: Organic farmer clusters (min. 20 farmers over 50 acres).

Details: Encourages sustainable organic farming, provides subsidies for organic raw materials, bio-fertilizers, and certification fees up to ₹50,000 per hectare.

Learn More & Apply

National Food Security Mission (NFSM)

Objective: Increase production of essential crops (rice, wheat, pulses, coarse cereals) to ensure food security.

Beneficiaries: Farmers of all categories (small, marginal, and large-scale).

Details: Provides financial and technical support for high-quality seeds, fertilizers, irrigation, soil health, and eco-friendly practices.

Learn More & Apply

Sub-Mission on Agricultural Mechanization (SMAM)

Objective: Provide subsidies on essential agricultural machinery to improve productivity.

Beneficiaries: Farmers.

Details: Offers subsidies on tractors, harvesters, and other planting machinery.

Learn More & Apply

Rashtriya Krishi Vikas Yojana (RKVY)

Objective: Financial assistance and grants for purchasing advanced farm equipment and construction.

Beneficiaries: Farmers.

Details: Fosters India's agricultural economy through mechanization and infrastructure development.

Learn More & Apply

State-Specific Schemes

Mukhya Mantri Kisan Kalyan Yojana (Madhya Pradesh)

Objective: Provides direct financial assistance to farmers.

Beneficiaries: Farmers in Madhya Pradesh.

Details: Often supplements the central government's PM-KISAN scheme.

Learn More & Apply

Bhavantar Bhugtan Yojana (Madhya Pradesh)

Objective: Price deficiency payment scheme to protect farmers from market price fluctuations.

Beneficiaries: Farmers in Madhya Pradesh.

Details: Compensates farmers when market prices fall below a predetermined support price.

Learn More & Apply

Mukhya Mantri Krishi Sinchai Yojana (Madhya Pradesh)

Objective: Expand irrigation facilities and promote water conservation.

Beneficiaries: Farmers in Madhya Pradesh. [13]

Details: Aims to bring more land under irrigation by creating new projects and renovating existing ones.

Learn More & Apply

Mukhyamantri Machhua Samridhi Yojana (Madhya Pradesh)

Objective: Invest in aquaculture, fish trade infrastructure, and skill development.

Beneficiaries: Fishing community in Madhya Pradesh.

Details: Supports livelihood improvement for the fishing community.
